Ukrainian Grammar Guide

The Alphabet

Ukrainian uses the Cyrillic script and has 33 letters.

Vowels

А а /a/ — like a in father

Е е /e/ — like e in bed

И и /ɪ/ — like i in bit (slightly darker)

І і /i/ — like ee in see

О о /o/ — like o in more

У у /u/ — like oo in boot

Я я /ja/ — like ya in yard

Є є /je/ — like ye in yes

Ї ї /ji/ — like yi in yield

Ю ю /ju/ — like yu in yule

Consonants

Б б /b/ · В в /v/ · Г г /ɦ/ (voiced h) · Ґ ґ /g/ · Д д /d/ · Ж ж /ʒ/ (like s in pleasure)

З з /z/ · К к /k/ · Л л /l/ · М м /m/ · Н н /n/ · П п /p/

Р р /r/ (rolled) · С с /s/ · Т т /t/ · Ф ф /f/ · Х х /x/ (like ch in Scottish loch)

Ц ц /ts/ · Ч ч /tʃ/ (like ch in church) · Ш ш /ʃ/ (like sh in show) · Щ щ /ʃtʃ/

Й й /j/ (like y in yes)

Special Signs

Ь ь — Soft sign: softens the preceding consonant (no sound of its own).

ʼ — Apostrophe: separates a hard consonant from a following iotated vowel (Я, Є, Ї, Ю).

Stress

Ukrainian stress is free and mobile — it can fall on any syllable and shifts within word forms. Stressed vowels are pronounced clearly; unstressed vowels are only slightly reduced.

Grammatical Gender

Every Ukrainian noun has one of three genders:

Masculine — typically ends in a consonant or -о: брат (brother), батько (father).

Feminine — typically ends in -а / -я: сестра (sister), земля (land).

Neuter — typically ends in -о / -е / -я: місто (city), море (sea).

Adjectives and pronouns must agree in gender with their noun.

Plural

Nouns change their ending in the plural. Common patterns:

брат → брати (brothers) · сестра → сестри (sisters) · місто → міста (cities)

After numbers: 1 = nominative singular; 2–4 = genitive singular; 5+ = genitive plural.

Example: 1 будинок, 2 будинки, 5 будинків (1 house, 2 houses, 5 houses)

The Seven Cases

Ukrainian nouns, pronouns and adjectives decline in 7 cases:

Nominative — subject: Хлопець іде. (The boy walks.)

Genitive — possession / absence: книга хлопця (the boy's book)

Dative — indirect object: дати хлопцеві (to give to the boy)

Accusative — direct object: бачити хлопця (to see the boy)

Instrumental — with / by means of: із хлопцем (with the boy)

Locative — location (always with preposition): про хлопця (about the boy)

Vocative — direct address: Хлопче! (Hey, boy!)

Personal Pronouns

я — I · ти — you (informal) · він — he · вона — she · воно — it

ми — we · ви — you (plural / formal) · вони — they

Ukrainian does not require a subject pronoun — the verb ending already shows the person.

Verb Conjugation

Verbs agree with the subject in person and number (present/future) and in gender and number (past tense).

Example — говорити (to speak), present tense:

я говорю — I speak

ти говориш — you speak

він/вона говорить — he/she speaks

ми говоримо — we speak

ви говорите — you speak (plural)

вони говорять — they speak

Verb Aspect

Every Ukrainian verb has an imperfective (ongoing/repeated action) and a perfective (completed action) form:

писати (imperfective — to be writing) ↔ написати (perfective — to write/finish writing)

Use imperfective for habits and ongoing actions; perfective for one-time completed actions.

Adjective Agreement

Adjectives agree with nouns in gender, number, and case.

великий (big, masc.) · велика (big, fem.) · велике (big, neut.) · великі (big, pl.)

Negation

Add не before the verb: Я не знаю. (I don't know.)

Double negation is standard: Я нічого не бачу. (I don't see anything. lit. I nothing not see.)

Question Formation

Questions are formed by intonation alone (no auxiliary verb needed):

Ти розумієш? — Do you understand?

Common question words: хто (who) · що (what) · де (where) · коли (when) · чому (why) · як (how) · скільки (how much/many)
